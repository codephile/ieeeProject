ltv corp said it has received a contract worth more than 500 mln dlrs from boeing co to produce major sections for boeing 767, 757 and 747 airliners, including the new 747-400. the company said the contract covers continuing production through mid-1990. ltv makes aft body sections and horizontal and vertical stabilizers for 747 and 757's and horizontal stabilizers for 767's. it said as part of the contract it will build a new type of horizontal stabilizer for the 747-400 containing tanks capable of holding 3,300 gallons of fuel. the added fuel capacity will allow the 747-400 to fly about 1,000 miles further than the 747-300, ltv said. the company said it will start making the 747-400 shipsets in june and the first aircraft is scheduled for completion in early 1988. it is to be delivered to nwa inc in december 1988. reuter 
