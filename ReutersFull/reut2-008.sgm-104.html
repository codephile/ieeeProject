a meeting of eight latin american coffee producers ended with a call for continued talks aimed at arriving at an agreement to stabilize international prices. a statement delivered by the conference's president, nicaragua's external trade minister alejandro martinez cuenca, said the object of future meetings would be to seek negotiations leading to basic export quotas. the meeting was attended by representatives from brazil, mexico, guatemala, el salvador, honduras, costa rica and nicaragua. a panamanian representative attended the meeting as an observer. representatives at the meeting said efforts would continue to be made to reach a regional consensus on export quotas which could be presented to the international coffee organization. in opening the meeting, nicaraguan president daniel ortega said the lack of an accord on export quotas was behind falling coffee prices, which he said have caused billions of dollars in losses to countries in the region. jorio dauster, president of the brazilian coffee institute, said his government is committed to working towards an producers accord in order to bring about higher prices. reuter 
