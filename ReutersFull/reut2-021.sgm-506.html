toyota motor corp and volkswagen ag are considering raising the local procurement rate at their west german joint venture to at least 60 pct from an earlier planned 50 pct after one year's output, a toyota spokesman said. the two carmakers agreed last june to start jointly producing a toyota-designed light truck at vw's hanover plant from early 1989 at a rate of 7,000 to 8,000 the first year, rising to 15,000 from 1990. they may equip the light truck with a volkswagen-made engine to reach a local procurement rate of 60 pct, he said. the two companies need to raise the value of locally procured parts to 60 pct to avoid tariffs when they export to other european markets, industry sources said. reuter 
