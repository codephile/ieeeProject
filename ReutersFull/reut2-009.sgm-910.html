jaguar cars inc, a subsidiary of jaguar plc, said it introduced its new 1988 xj6 luxury sedan to replace its series iii xj6 sedan. the 40,500-dlr car will be available in showrooms starting may four, the company said. many parts of the xj6 and the 44,500 dlr vanden plas have been changed, adding high-tech features. the car has seven micro-processors that control its major electrical functions. the in-car computer offers a wide array of trip information. the vanden plas offers more features than the basic xj6. jaguar said it has a "substantial waiting list" for the cars in europe, where it was introduced last october. reuter 
