delegates from across latin america and the caribbean opened a five-day meeting here today aimed at mapping a regional strategy for this year's meeting of the united nations conference on trade and development (unctad). representatives from 26 nations are participating in the talks here, which well-placed sources said would focus on latin america's staggering foreign debt and efforts to pull the region out of its worst economic nosedive in more than half a century. unctad's annual meeting is scheduled for july in geneva. in april developing countries belonging to the so-called group of 77 will attempt to hammer out a common position for the unctad conference at a preparatory session in havana, cuba. reuter 
