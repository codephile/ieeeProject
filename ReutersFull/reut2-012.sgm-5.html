insurance group guardian royal exchange plc said it would expand its life assurance business and push further into european markets, but that u.k. underwriting losses remained a heavy drag on profits. earlier, the group had announced sharply increased pre-tax profits of 143.8 mln stg for calendar 1986, versus 3.5 mln the previous year. "we are anxious to develop our life assurance business," group managing director peter dugdale told a news conference. "if we have a thrust for the coming years, it is probably best to spend more time on life in the u.k. and abroad," he said. reuter 
