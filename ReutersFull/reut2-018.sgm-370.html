the main navigation channel of the rhine, blocked by two sunken boats, will remain closed at least until the beginning of next week, a water authority spokesman said today. karlsruhe water authorities said a queue of about 70 ships had formed between the towns of speyer and iffezheim but most shipping companies had unloaded cargoes and were using alternative means of transport. heinz-josef recker, of the mannheim water authority, said two floating cranes would make a fresh attempt to raise the sunken tug and its lighter after an unsuccessful try on friday. the tug orinoko hit a railway bridge last tuesday and sank with a cargo of 3,500 tonnes of gravel on board. before the accident, about 100 ships a day sailed under the bridge. reuter 
