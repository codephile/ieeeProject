republicans apparently have ended their vote boycott at the budget drafting sessions of the house budget committee. house budget committee chairman william gray, a democrat, announced that republicans now would like to join in the effort to write a budget, but behind closed doors. last week, republicans refused to vote on any budget issues contending they had been left out of the process. the committee plans to meet tomorrow on a budget and gray said he welcomes the "good faith effort" of the minority republicans. reuter 
