prime minister margaret thatcher said the u.k. was growing more impatient with japanese trade barriers and warned that it would soon have new powers against countries not offering reciprocal access to their markets. she told parliament that the bid by the u.k.'s cable and wireless plc to enter the japanese telecommunications market was being regarded by her government as a test case. "i wrote to the prime minister of japan, mr nakasone, on the fourth of march to express our interest on the cable and wireless bid. i have not yet had a reply. we see this as a test on how open the japanese market really is," thatcher said. thatcher told parliament that "shortly ... we shall have more powers than we have now, when, for example the powers under the financial services act and the banking act become available, then we shall be able to take action in cases where other countries do not offer the same full access to financial services as we do." cable and wireless is seeking a stake in the proposed japanese telecommunications rival to kokusai denshin denwa. but the japanese minister for post and telecommunications was reported as saying that he opposed cable and wireless having a managerial role in the new company. reuter 
