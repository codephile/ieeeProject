trinidad and tobago is finalizing arrangements with union carbide of the united states and snamprogetti of italy for the construction of a 1,500 tonnes per day methanol plant, energy minister kelvin ramnath said. ramnath said the ministry is now holding talks with union carbide on the price of natural gas to be used in the plant, which will be constructed near the trinidad and tobago oil company (trintoc) refinery at point fortin on the west coast. snamprogetti built the first methanol refinery on trinidad five years ago. trintoc is likely to put up land, refinery plant and machinery as equity. if negotiations go smoothly, ramnath said, construction could begin by next january. the government of prime minister a.n.r. robinson is hoping to lue new investors to the twin-island state's petrochemical industry, in order to make use of new findings of natural gas. reuter 
