boeing co's boeing commerical airplane co said it selected an unducted fan engine for continued development as the baseline engine for the advanced technology 7j7 airliner due to enter service in 1992. boeing said it reached the decision following a three-month study of superfan concept and the general electric udf engine. boeing said the udf engine would up with a nine pct advantage in fuel savings over the superfan on a typical 500-nautical-mile trip. boeing said it wil proceed with detail design of the 7j7 and could launch the program in 1987 depending on the market response. it will seat about 150 passengers, the company said. reuter 
