commitments to argentina's external financing program for 1987 and 1988 have reached about 91 pct of the 1.95 billion dlrs in new loans that banks have been asked to provide, citibank and argentina said in a joint statement. this percentage is close to the "critical mass" of commitments that the international monetary fund has requested as a condition for releasing new loans to the country. senior citibank executive william rhodes said he was encouraged by the banks' response and that telexed commitments continue to arrive. the response of argentina's 350 foreign bank creditors had been eagerly awaited because the package is the first to be marketed since u.s. banks, led by citibank, decided to take large second-quarter losses in order to boost their reserves against existing loans to developing countries. some bankers had feared that banks would refuse to lend new money so soon after taking a "hit" on the old loans, but today's news delivers a decisive rebuff to those skeptics. the strong response to the package was partly the result of the inducement of a 3/8 pct early-participation fee for those banks that agreed to sign up by june 17, bankers said. reuter 
