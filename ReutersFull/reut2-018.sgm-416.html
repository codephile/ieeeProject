the agricultural stabilization and conservation service announced several temporary steps to enable it to respond more quickly to the high demand for commodity credit corp.-owned soybeans. the ccc has suspended its practice of contacting storing warehousemen before selling soybeans for cash to a third party, said collyn peterson, deputy director in the ascs kansas city office. in addition, he said, minimum quantities for sales will be 25,000 bushels, or all of a warehouse total inventory if less than that. ascs has added telephone lines to handle calls from those interested in buying soybeans, peterson said. reuter 
