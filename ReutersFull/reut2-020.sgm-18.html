about 60,000 tonnes of harvested raw cotton may be spoiled by rain in tanzania's northern shinyanga region because it is stored in the open or in crude village sheds, radio tanzania reported. the cotton, worth one billion shillings, cannot be moved to ginneries in the region because most mill warehouses are full. many mills are not working because of a lack of spare parts, it added. agriculture ministry officials have forecast a 1987/88 cotton harvest of about 200,000 tonnes, down from 215,000 in 1986/87. reuter 
