west german retail group kaufhof ag is considering taking a stake in shipping and transport group hapag-lloyd ag but has yet to reach a final decision, a spokesman said in response to queries. press reports said kaufhof wanted a stake of up to 12.5 pct in hapag-lloyd. the kaufhof spokesman noted any decision on purchasing shares in the shipping group would have to be approved by the supervisory board, which is due to hold a meeting tomorrow. late last year the gevaert group of belgium and west germany's veba ag said they had each acquired a 12.5 pct stake in hapag-lloyd from deutsche bank ag and dresdner bank ag . industry sources estimate deutsche and dresdner, hapag-lloyd's majority shareholders, held about 75 pct of hapag-lloyd's share capital before selling portions of it to gevaert and veba. the two banks have said they eventually wanted to reduce their stake in the shipping group to 15 pct each. reuter 
