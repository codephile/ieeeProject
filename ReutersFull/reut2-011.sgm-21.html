the u.s. senate has approved a measure that would allow an estimated 4,200 agricultural banks to write off loan losses over ten years. the measure, offered by sen. alan dixon (d-ill.), was attached to a bill to recapitalize the federal savings and loan insurance corp that the senate approved march 27. dixon's amendment would permit agricultural banks -- banks with assets of 100 mln dlrs or less and at least 25 pct of total loans in farm loans -- to write down over ten years agricultural loan losses incurred between 1984 and 1992. reuter 
