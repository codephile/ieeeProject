the reagan administration protested for the first time the constitutionality of the 1978 special prosecutor law that led to a current investigation of the iran-contra scandal. the constitutional attack by assistant attorney general john bolton was the first official public objection, although reportedly the administration privately has opposed the ethics in government act. bolton told a senate subcommittee the administration "believes there are grave constitutional problems" with the law that sets up court-appointed independent prosecutors, or independent counsels, to probe high ranking government officials. reuter 
