le matin, the french-language tabloid launched here in february, said it is shutting down its operations because it can no longer get financing. the paper, launched by private investors, was montreal's fourth french-language newspaper and was aimed at readers with above average incomes and educations. it had a daily circulation of about 20,000 copies. le matin was printed and distributed by (southam inc)'s montreal gazette newspaper. a gazette spokesman said his newspaper is le matin's biggest creditor but he declined to reveal the amount of debt acting publisher jean-pierre bordua said the newspaper's bank blocked its lines of credit after three of the paper's senior managers resigned friday. reuter 
