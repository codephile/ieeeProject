the tanzanian government, in its second annual budget since embarking on an economic recovery program inspired by the international monetary fund, announced increases in fuel prices and sales tax and higher levies on government services. finance minister cleopa msuya said regular petrol would go up 52 pct, with similar increases in the cost of premium and kerosene. diesel would rise by 75 pct. msuya said the government expected to spend 77.33 billion shillings in the financial year 1987/8, 39 pct more than this year's estimate. revenue would provide 48.84 billion shillings, leaving a deficit of 28.49 billion to be financed through domestic and foreign loans. reuter 
