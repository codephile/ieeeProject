u.s. agriculture undersecretary daniel amstutz said he was confident that a european community, ec, proposal to tax vegetable and marine oils and fats would not be approved by the community. asked by reuters if he was confident the plan eventually would be defeated, amstutz said, "yes, i am, but i also know that things like this take constant vigilance." the usda official said the ec council of ministers held an ad hoc meeting yesterday. commenting on that meeting, he said, "so far, so good." yesterday british farm minister michael jopling said enough ec states appeared to be opposed to the proposals by the commission for a 330 european currency unit a tonne tax on vegetable oils, fats and marine oils for the moves to be defeated. reuter 
