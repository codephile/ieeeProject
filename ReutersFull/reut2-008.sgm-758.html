rice and corn farmers will receive the largest payments from the u.s. government during 1987 if the subsidies are calculated per planted acre, the u.s. agriculture department said. usda said government outlays to rice farmers in 1987 are expected to reach 403 dlrs per acre planted, followed by corn at 135 dlrs per planted acre. government outlays include mainly deficiency payments and price support loans. on a per acre basis, cotton payments will reach 73.24 dlrs in 1987, wheat 60.30 dlrs, sorghum 54.38 dlrs, barley 27.41 dlrs and oats 2.31 dlrs, usda said. usda estimates farm subsidies will reach 25.3 billion dlrs in 1987. the figures were given by usda officials at a senate agriculture appropriations hearing yesterday. reuter 
