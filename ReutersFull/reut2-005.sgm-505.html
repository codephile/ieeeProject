u.s. trade representative clayton yeutter said the united states will retaliate if the european community adopts a proposed tax on vegetable oils, but he did not say what ec products would be singled-out for reprisal. speaking to the national grain and feed association (ngfa) convention here, yeutter said he is "cautiously optimistic" the controversial oils tax proposal will be rejected by the ec council of ministers. yeutter said the proposed tax is a "flagrant violation of the spirit of gatt" because it would imperil the zero binding duty on u.s. exports of soybeans to europe. he said the reagan administration has yet to decide on a retaliation list. the administration would not reveal a retaliation list unless the ec approved the proposal, he said. reuter 
