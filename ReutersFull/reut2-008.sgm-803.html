e-tron corp said it will submit a settlement agreement reached with its former president to the bankruptcy court for approval. it said the settlement stems from a suit against the former company president. e-tron, which is in chapter 11, said the settlement calls for irwin lampert to return 2,350,000 shares of e-tron stock to its treasury, and prohibits lampert from starting a business competive with e-tron, the company said. it added irwin must repay certain debts, also. e-tron said it will submit soon a creditor proposal plan to get out of chapter 11. reuter 
