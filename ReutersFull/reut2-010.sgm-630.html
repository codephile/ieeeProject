a tentative settlement has been reached in a three-day strike by 9,000 workers at a general motors truck and bus complex, a union spokesman said. united auto workers (uaw) spokesman don hooper told reuters in a telephone interview a settlement had been reached and a ratification vote was expected today. he had no further details. gm officials were unavailable for comment, but the head of security at one of the struck plants confirmed a tentative settlement of the dispute. the uaw struck at three gm factories on thursday as labor contract talks broke down on the issue of using non-union labour on subcontracting jobs. about 9,000 hourly workers walked off the job after the uaw and management failed to come to an agreement by thursday noon, a deadline set last week by the union. reuter 
