b.c. resources' 1986 extraordinary charge also included a writedown of its investment in westar petroleum. gains on the sales of westar timber's skeena and celgar pulp mills and terrace sawmill partially offset the extraordinary loss, the company said. the company said the asset sales have eliminated b.c. resources' long term financing commitment in the north sea and exposure to the fluctuating pulp market. it also said it cut long term debt in 1986 to 900 mln dlrs from 1.3 billion dlrs at year-end 1985, and management changes and staff cuts have significantly reduced costs.
