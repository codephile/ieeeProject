lloyd's of london, the insurance market, said its offer for a settlement of the five-year old affair, which involved losses totaling about 235 mln stg, was unconditional. for the offers to become unconditional, there had to be 90 pct acceptance by those lloyd's members affected by the pcw fraud, although this level could be reduced by a caretaker agency set up in 1985 to manage the affair. the offers involve one-off cash payments by pcw names - investors who guarantee insurance risks - in exchange for a lloyd's guarantee ending their exposure to pcw-related claims. reuter 
