vietnam will resettle 300,000 people on state farms known as new economic zones in 1987, to create jobs and grow more high-value export crops, the communist party newspaper nhan dan said. yesterday's edition, received here today, said vietnam would invest one billion dong, including the costs of relocation, in 272 new economic zones. about one third of that sum would be spent on export crops such as coffee, tea, rubber and pepper in the central highlands, it said. since 1975, vietnam has resettled about three million people from cities and crowded river deltas to the zones. reuter 
